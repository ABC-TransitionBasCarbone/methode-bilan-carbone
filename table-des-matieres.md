## Step 1 - Scoping the approach

Every organisation must start with this step, which frames the Bilan Carbone® approach and clearly defines what needs to be carried out in the subsequent stages, as the approach adapts to the organisation and its objectives.&#x20;

* [1.1 - Define your maturity level](1-cadrage-de-la-demarche/1.1-definir-son-niveau-de-maturite-bilan-carbone-r.md) in terms of carbon accounting: As this methodology is structured around three maturity levels for organisations carrying out their Bilan Carbone®, this sub-section helps select the appropriate level.&#x20;
* [1.2 - Define the governance and coordination of the approach](1-cadrage-de-la-demarche/1.2-definir-le-pilotage-de-la-demarche-bilan-carbone-r.md) : This step aims to establish the internal governance necessary for the successful completion of the approach and the achievement of the chosen maturity criteria.

## Step 2 - Scope of the approach

The organisation must define several boundaries that delimit the study. This makes it possible to prepare the accounting phase, ensuring that all direct and indirect emissions from the organisation are included.

* [2 - Introduction to scope identification](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2-introduction-a-lidentification-des-perimetres.md) : This introductory sub-section presents the timeline of the step, as well as the definitions, requirements, and expected governance for Step 2.
* [2.1 - Emissions accounted for in a Bilan Carbone](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2.1-les-emissions-comptabilisees-dans-un-bilan-carbone-r.md)® : This sub-section presents the different emission pillars of an organisation: induced emissions, avoided emissions and sequestered emissions.&#x20;
* [2.2 - Identification of the organisational boundary](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2.2-perimetre-organisationnel.md) : This sub-section recalls that this methodological guide applies at the organisational level, and frames the organisational boundary.
* [2.3 - Identification of the temporal boundary](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2.3-perimetre-temporel.md) : This sub-section sets the period(s) over which emissions are accounted for.
* [2.4 - Identification of the operational boundary](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2.4-perimetre-operationnel.md) based on the identification of emission sources
* [2.5 - Identification of transition risks and opportunities](2-perimetre-de-la-demarche/2.4-perimetre-operationnel.md) : This sub-section presents different types of risks and opportunities related to climate change that may affect the organisation, and the various requirements for analysing them.

  * [3.1.3 : for the construction of the GHG profile](3-mobilisation-des-parties-prenantes/3.1-programmer-les-phases-de-mobilisation/3.1.3-a-lissue-de-la-construction-du-profil-ges.md)
  * [3.1.4 : for the construction of the transition plan](3-mobilisation-des-parties-prenantes/3.1-programmer-les-phases-de-mobilisation/3.1.4-pour-la-construction-du-plan-de-transition.md)

## Step 4 - Accounting

This involves drawing up the organisation's emission profile and obtaining the quantified result of the Bilan Carbone®. The quality of the accounting adapts to the organisation and its resources based on its maturity level.

* [4 - Introduction to emissions accounting](4-comptabilisation/4.1-methode-de-quantification-des-emissions.md) : This introductory sub-section presents the timeline of the step, as well as the definitions, requirements, and expected governance for Step 4.
* [4.1 - Emissions quantification method:](4-comptabilisation/4.1-methode-de-quantification-des-emissions.md) This consists of collecting all required activity data and converting it into tonnes of CO2 equivalent using emission factors, while estimating the associated uncertainty.
* [4.2 - Activity data collection method:](4-comptabilisation/4.2-methode-de-collecte-des-donnees-dactivite.md) This sub-section details the activity data collection approaches available to an organisation, and distinguishes acceptable methods according to the organisation's maturity level.
* [4.3 - Emission factor selection method](4-comptabilisation/4.3-methode-de-selection-des-facteurs-demission.md) : This sub-section details the approaches for selecting the emission factors the organisation will use, and the expectations for the different maturity levels.&#x20;
* [4.4 - Uncertainty estimation method](4-comptabilisation/4.4-methode-destimation-des-incertitudes/) : This sub-section details the method for estimating uncertainties associated with emissions, and the expectations for the different maturity levels.
* [4.5 - Emission results and profiles ](4-comptabilisation/4.5-profil-demission.md): This involves drawing up the organisation's emission profile across several possible result export formats, providing equivalences.

## Step 5 - Transition plan

A transition plan must be drawn up following the accounting phase. A series of indicators enables the implementation of these actions to be monitored.&#x20;

* [5 - Developing a transition plan](5-plan-de-transition/5-introduction-au-plan-de-transition.md) : This introductory sub-section presents the timeline of the step, as well as the definitions, requirements, and expected governance for Step 5.
* [5.1 - Definition of objectives](5-plan-de-transition/5.1-definition-des-objectifs.md) : This sub-section details the expectations in terms of transition strategy for the different maturity levels.
* [5.2 - Construction of the action plan](5-plan-de-transition/5.2-construction-du-plan-daction.md) : This sub-section presents the different types of actions to be included in the transition plan, by maturity level.
* [5.3 - Definition of the transition pathway](5-plan-de-transition/5.3-definition-de-la-trajectoire-de-transition.md) : The notion of pathway and the expectations for the different maturity levels are presented in this sub-section.
* [5.4 - Implementation of the transition plan](5-plan-de-transition/5.4-mise-en-oeuvre-du-plan-de-transition.md) : This sub-section provides advice to follow for correctly implementing the transition plan.
* [5.5 - Monitoring and management of the transition plan](5-plan-de-transition/5.5-suivi-et-pilotage-du-plan-de-transition.md) : The different types of indicators useful for managing actions are described here. The expectations in terms of indicators, management and monitoring of actions are detailed for the different maturity levels.&#x20;

## Step 6 - Summary and reporting

The result of a Bilan Carbone® is the quantification of the organisation's GHG emissions, broken down by emission category within the considered boundaries, along with at least one coherently proposed transition plan and the associated monitoring indicators.

* [6 - Introduction to approach reporting](6-synthese-et-restitution/6-introduction-a-la-restitution-de-la-demarche.md) : This introductory sub-section presents the timeline of the step, as well as the definitions, requirements, and expected governance for Step 6.
* [6.1 - Bilan Carbone® report](6-synthese-et-restitution/6.1-restitution-du-bilan-carbone-r.md) : This sub-section indicates how to report on the Bilan Carbone® approach and what deliverables are expected (covering the engagement, accounting and transition plan phases) based on the chosen maturity level.
* [6.2 - Compatibility of the approach with other frameworks ](6-synthese-et-restitution/6.2-compatibilite-de-la-demarche-avec-dautres-referentiels.md): This sub-section enables extraction of the information necessary to meet the requirements of different reporting methods and submission frameworks.
* [6.3 - Renewal and continuous improvement](6-synthese-et-restitution/6.3-renouvellement-et-amelioration-continue.md) : This sub-section briefly addresses how to organise the renewal of the approach, and how to go further and improve once the reporting is complete, regardless of the maturity level.&#x20;
